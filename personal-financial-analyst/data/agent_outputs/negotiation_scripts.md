### 1. Internet - Comcast — Current: $89.99/month

#### Market Research

**Competitor Prices (same area):**
- AT&T Fiber 500Mbps: $55.00/month (12-month promo)
- Google Fiber 1Gbps: $70.00/month
- T-Mobile Home Internet: $50.00/month (fixed, no contract)
- Comcast new customer promo: $49.99/month (same service tier)

**Leverage Points:**
- Comcast new-customer rate is 44% cheaper than your current rate — same company, same service
- T-Mobile Home Internet is $50/month with no contract or equipment fees
- You are paying the post-promotion "loyalty tax" — customers who don't call pay ~$30–50/month more
- Comcast retention department has authority to match competitor pricing

#### Negotiation Script

**Best time to call:** Tuesday–Thursday, 10am–2pm (lowest hold times, retention agents have more flexibility)

**Opening Statement:**
> "Hi, I've been a Comcast customer for several years and I want to talk about my current bill. I'm paying $89.99/month and I've received offers from T-Mobile Home Internet at $50/month and AT&T Fiber at $55/month for faster speeds. I'd like to keep my service with Comcast, but I need my bill to be more in line with what competitors are offering. Can you help me with that?"

**Present Alternatives:**
> "I have a written offer here from T-Mobile for $50/month — no contract, no equipment rental. AT&T Fiber is offering $55/month for 500Mbps. I'd prefer to stay with Comcast, but not at $90 a month when I can get faster service elsewhere for $40 less."

**Target Price:** $54.99/month
**Acceptable Range:** $49.99–$64.99/month

**Handle Objections:**

**If they say:** "I can't lower your rate, but I can add a premium channel package."
**You respond:** "I appreciate that, but I'm focused on the monthly bill, not additional services. I don't use premium channels. Can you connect me to your retention department?"

**If they say:** "That promotion is for new customers only."
**You respond:** "I understand, but I've been a loyal customer. T-Mobile is offering me $50/month right now as a new customer. Are you saying Comcast would rather lose me to a competitor than match a reasonable rate for someone who's already with you?"

**If they say:** "The best I can do is $74.99."
**You respond:** "That's still $25 more than the T-Mobile offer. I'd like to stay, but I need it at $59.99 or lower. Is there a supervisor or retention specialist I can speak with?"

**Closing:**
- **If accepted:** "Great, can you confirm that rate in writing via email? I'd also like to confirm the new monthly amount, any contract terms, and when it takes effect."
- **If rejected:** Cancel service or escalate to retention — say "I'll need to cancel and switch to T-Mobile. Can you process a cancellation? I understand there may be a cancellation offer at that point." (Cancellation calls almost always generate a retention offer.)

#### Expected Savings
- **Target Monthly:** $54.99 (from $89.99)
- **Monthly Savings:** $35.00
- **Annual Savings:** $420.00
- **Success Probability:** 85% (Comcast retention saves ~80-90% of callers who threaten to cancel)

### 2. Car Insurance - State Farm — Current: $185.00/month

#### Market Research

**Competitor Quotes (estimated for similar profile):**
- GEICO: ~$145–155/month
- Progressive: ~$138–150/month
- Allstate: ~$165–175/month
- USAA (if eligible): ~$110–130/month

**Leverage Points:**
- Competitors offer 10–25% lower premiums for equivalent coverage
- State Farm offers loyalty discounts — ask explicitly if you're receiving them
- Bundling home + auto saves 5–15%
- Annual payment (vs. monthly) typically saves 3–8%
- Telematics programs (Drive Safe & Save) can cut premiums 10–30%

#### Negotiation Script

**Best approach:** Call your State Farm agent directly (not the 1-800 line) — agents have discretion on discounts.

**Opening Statement:**
> "Hi [Agent Name], I've been a State Farm customer for [X years] and I want to review my auto policy. I've been getting quotes from competitors and I'm seeing rates 15–20% lower than what I'm currently paying. Before I switch, I want to see if we can work something out — I'd like to stay with State Farm."

**Present Alternatives:**
> "GEICO quoted me $147/month for the same coverage. Progressive came in at $142. I'm currently paying $185. That's a $40–45/month difference. Is there a review or discount I might be missing?"

**Target Price:** $145.00/month
**Acceptable Range:** $140–$160/month

**Handle Objections:**

**If they say:** "Your rate is based on your driving record and coverage level."
**You respond:** "I understand the rating factors. I'm asking specifically about available discounts — loyalty discount, multi-policy, telematics, good driver — am I receiving all of them? Can you run a discount audit on my policy?"

**If they say:** "I can't match those competitor quotes."
**You respond:** "I appreciate your honesty. In that case, I'd like to request a policy review and then I'll need to move forward with one of the competitors. Can you prepare the cancellation paperwork?"

**Closing:**
- **If discounts applied:** "Thank you. Please send me confirmation of the new rate and when it takes effect."
- **If switching:** Get competing quote in writing, cancel State Farm with 30-day notice to avoid coverage gaps.

#### Expected Savings
- **Target Monthly:** $150.00 (from $185.00)
- **Monthly Savings:** $35.00
- **Annual Savings:** $420.00
- **Success Probability:** 60% (insurance is harder to negotiate than telecom, but discount audits often reveal missed savings)

### 3. New York Times — Current: $29.99/month

#### Market Research

**Competitor Prices:**
- Wall Street Journal: $17.99/month (digital)
- Washington Post: $10.00/month
- The Atlantic: $9.99/month
- NYT own promotions for win-back: often $1–4/month for first 6 months

**Leverage Points:**
- NYT frequently runs 50–80% discounts for customers who call to cancel
- Annual plan reduces to $17/month effective
- The $29.99 all-access tier includes Cooking, Games, Wirecutter — assess actual usage

#### Negotiation Script

**Opening Statement:**
> "Hi, I'm considering canceling my New York Times subscription. I've been a subscriber for [X time] but $29.99 a month is more than I can justify right now. I'm looking at the Washington Post for $10/month. Before I cancel, is there anything you can do on the rate?"

**Present Alternatives:**
> "Washington Post is $10/month, The Atlantic is $10/month. The New York Times is triple those prices. I'd like to stay, but only at a lower rate."

**Target Price:** $9.99/month or free for 3 months
**Acceptable Range:** $9.99–$17.00/month

**Handle Objections:**

**If they say:** "Your subscription includes full access to Cooking, Games, and Wirecutter."
**You respond:** "I understand, but I'm primarily a news reader. I'd be happy to downgrade to a News-only plan at a lower rate."

**Closing:**
- **If offer given:** Confirm the duration (many are promotional 3–6 month rates; ask what it reverts to)
- **If rejected:** Cancel and wait 2–4 weeks — NYT win-back campaigns typically offer $0.50–$1.00/week

#### Expected Savings

- **Success Probability:** 90% (digital news subscriptions almost always offer retention discounts)
